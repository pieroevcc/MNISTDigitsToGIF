# v1 — EMA + Bigger Model Run (base 96, EMA, bf16)

Second full training run, the planned follow-up to v0. Same data/schedule as the baseline
so the comparison is apples-to-apples; the only differences are EMA sampling, a larger
U-Net (`--base 96`), and bf16 autocast. Samples are drawn from the EMA weights.

## Hardware
- GPU: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060 Laptop GPU (8 GB)
- PyTorch 2.12.1+cu126, Python 3.12.10, Windows 11

## Configuration
| Param | Value | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| `--size` | 32 | frame H×W |
| `--frames` | 10 | clip length T |
| `--digits` | 1 | one bouncing digit per clip |
| `--base` | 96 | U-Net base channels (was 64) |
| `--mults` | 1 2 4 | channel multipliers |
| `--timesteps` | 1000 | DDPM diffusion steps |
| `--batch` | 16 | fits 8 GB VRAM |
| `--length` | 20000 | synthetic clips per epoch |
| `--epochs` | 30 | |
| `--lr` | 2e-4 | AdamW |
| Sampler | DDPM, full 1000-step reverse loop | sampled from EMA weights |
| EMA | yes (decay 0.999) | EMA weights used for sampling |
| Precision | bf16 autocast | fp16 overflowed base-96 activations to NaN |

Command: `python video_diffusion.py train --base 96 --ema`

## Results
- **Model size:** 89.4M params (`checkpoints/ckpt_epoch30.pt`, ~1.4 GB — checkpoint also
  stores the EMA shadow weights and optimizer state)
- **Architecture:** base-96 U-Net, ~2.25× the baseline's parameter count (39.7M → 89.4M)
- **VRAM:** comfortably within 8 GB at batch 16 under bf16
- **Final loss:** ~0.003–0.011 (oscillating), epoch-30 mean ≈ 0.0055

### Training notes
- **fp16 → bf16 fix.** The first attempt used fp16 autocast and ran cleanly through
  epoch 8, then **diverged to NaN at epoch 9, step ~10,150** (base-96 activations
  overflowed fp16's range; see the comment in `video_diffusion.py` `train()`). Switching
  to **bf16 autocast** — which keeps fp32's exponent range — resolved it; training was
  resumed from `ckpt_epoch8.pt` and completed all 30 epochs cleanly. The per-epoch table
  below uses the valid bf16 numbers for epochs 9–10 (the NaN steps are excluded).
- Resumed twice via `ckpt_epoch8.pt` and later `ckpt_latest.pt`; step counter is
  continuous (final step 37,500).

### Loss convergence (mean MSE per epoch)
| Epoch | 1 | 2 | 3 | 5 | 10 | 15 | 20 | 25 | 28 | 29 | 30 |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Loss | 0.0295 | 0.0138 | 0.0126 | 0.0110 | 0.0078 | 0.0068 | 0.0061 | 0.0060 | 0.0055 | 0.0057 | 0.0055 |

**Key observation:** like v0, the raw MSE drops fast in the first ~3 epochs and then
flattens. The larger model settles at a slightly **lower noise floor** (~0.0055 vs the
baseline's ~0.01-range late epochs) and keeps inching down through epoch ~28. As with v0,
the loss curve alone understates the quality gain — the visible improvement comes from the
extra capacity plus the smoother EMA weights, not from a dramatically lower MSE.

## Qualitative observations
- Per-epoch training previews (EMA samples): `samples_v1/sample_e{1..30}_{0..3}.gif`
- Same-seed comparison clips: `generations/v1_seed1234/`
- 2×4 v1-vs-v0 comparison grid: `generations/grid_v1_vs_v0_seed1234.gif`
- Per-digit 0–9 v1-vs-v0 grid: `generations/comparison_by_digit.gif`

Digits are recognizable and bounce/reflect correctly as in v0, but **strokes are
noticeably bolder and cleaner** with less of the thin/wispy, slightly-noisy texture that
limited the baseline. The EMA weights remove most of the per-step jitter, giving steadier,
less flickery clips.

## What changed vs v0
1. **EMA sampling (decay 0.999).** Samples come from an exponential moving average of the
   weights instead of the raw model — cleaner, less jittery output.
2. **Bigger model (`--base 96`).** ~2.25× parameters, 39.7M → 89.4M, for more capacity to
   render bold, sharp strokes. Drives the larger ~1.4 GB checkpoint.
3. **bf16 autocast (the fp16→NaN fix).** fp16 overflowed base-96 activations to NaN at
   epoch 9; bf16 keeps fp32's exponent range and trained stably to completion.

Everything else — `--size 32`, `--frames 10`, `--digits 1`, `--mults 1 2 4`,
`--timesteps 1000`, `--batch 16`, `--length 20000`, `--epochs 30`, `--lr 2e-4`, and the
full 1000-step DDPM sampler — is identical to v0.
